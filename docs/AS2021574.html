About Morocco

Morocco, a North African country bordering the Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea, is distinguished by its Berber, Arabian and European cultural influences. Marrakeshs medina, a mazelike medieval quarter, offers entertainment in its Djemaa el-Fna square and souks (marketplaces) selling ceramics, jewelry and metal lanterns. The capital Rabats Kasbah of the Udayas is a 12th-century royal fort overlooking the water.

  • Location & Geography

    Morocco is located in the Maghreb region of North Africa. It borders the Mediterranean Sea to the north, the Atlantic Ocean to the west, Algeria to the east, and the disputed territory of Western Sahara to the south. Morocco also claims Spanish exclaves Ceuta, Melilla, and several small islands off its coast. The capital is Rabat, and the largest city is Casablanca.

  • Population & Language

    Morocco has a population of roughly 37 million. The official and predominant religion is Islam. The official languages are Arabic and Berber, while French and the Moroccan dialect of Arabic are also widely spoken.

  • Cultural Identity

    Moroccan identity and culture are a blend of Arab, Berber, African, and European influences.

  • Political Structure

    Morocco is a unitary semi-constitutional monarchy with an elected parliament. The King of Morocco holds significant executive and legislative powers, especially over the military, foreign policy, and religious affairs. The executive branch is led by the King and the Prime Minister. Legislative power is vested in the House of Representatives and the House of Councillors. Judicial power rests with the Constitutional Court, which reviews the validity of laws, elections, and referendums.

  • Economic & Political Influence

    Morocco has the fifth-largest economy in Africa and wields significant influence in both Africa and the Arab world. It is considered a middle power in global affairs and holds membership in the Arab League, the Arab Maghreb Union, the Union for the Mediterranean, and the African Union.

Conclusion and Discussion

Conclusion

Based on the exploratory data analysis of COVID-19 data for Morocco, several important insights have been gained. The summary statistics and histograms indicate that the distributions of confirmed, death, and recovered cases are positively skewed, with mean values of 1113.29, 14.257, and 509.79 respectively. The time series analysis revealed four significant waves of confirmed cases, each followed by corresponding waves in death cases, although the magnitude of deaths remained significantly lower. The absence of recovered case data since mid-2021 poses a challenge in interpreting recovery trends. Additionally, Moroccos percentage of confirmed cases relative to its population is notably low compared to other African countries, this can be observed by both the bar chart and the heat map. Correlation analysis between total confirmed cases and population across African countries showed no relationship. These findings provide an overview of the COVID-19 situation in Morocco.This highlights the progression of the pandemic and the relatively low impact in terms of population percentage.
